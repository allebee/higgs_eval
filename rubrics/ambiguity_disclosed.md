# Rubric: Ambiguity disclosed

When the question is ambiguous (e.g., "the Voyager probe" could mean Voyager 1
or Voyager 2, both of which crossed the heliopause but at very different
dates), the agent should **either** ask for clarification **or** answer while
explicitly acknowledging the ambiguity and covering both interpretations.

## Pass criteria
- The answer mentions the ambiguity, OR
- The answer covers both plausible interpretations with their distinct facts.

## Fail criteria
- The answer silently picks one interpretation and presents it as if it were
  the only one (e.g., just says "2012" without mentioning Voyager 2's 2018
  crossing when the question says "the Voyager probe").
- The answer confuses the two probes.

## Partial
- The answer picks one but acknowledges the choice.
